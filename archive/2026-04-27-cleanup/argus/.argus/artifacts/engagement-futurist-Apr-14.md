## Futurist — Round 2 Engagement

**Responding to Pragmatist:**
Your call for "mass deletion" and merging `strategy_engine.py` and `backtest_engine.py` is the claim that most threatens my view, because it treats a symptom while cementing the disease. Trace this forward six months: if we just merge those files into a single entry point without changing the underlying representation of a strategy, we've only created a cleaner monolith of hardcoded Python logic that is still permanently severed from the Pine Script generator. Deleting the 3,500-line graveyard in `scripts/strategies.py` feels like momentum, but it ignores *why* it's a graveyard: human-written string templates cannot be autonomously mutated by the engine. If you merge the engines now to optimize for immediate simplicity, you make the eventual extraction of a shared Abstract Syntax Tree (AST) significantly harder, structurally locking us out of the North Star goal of programmatic logic discovery.

**Responding to Exploiter:**
I want to agree and extend your finding regarding the concurrent JSON state files and the string formatter. You noted that without atomic file locks, concurrent evolutionary scripts will inevitably corrupt the shared JSON state files. Trace this forward to when the team attempts to scale the optimization engine horizontally across a cluster to process the expanded search space. This isn't just an integrity risk; it is a hard, physical ceiling on our ability to scale compute. Furthermore, your identification of the string-formatter in `pine_generator.py` as an injection risk perfectly reinforces my architectural argument. Moving to a shared AST doesn't just enable automated strategy discovery; it fundamentally eliminates the injection surface area by treating strategy components as a compiled data structure rather than concatenated raw strings.

Reading the Craftsman's analysis of the fractured domain model (`Trade` and `BacktestResult` defined twice across the engines) has shifted my view on sequencing.
