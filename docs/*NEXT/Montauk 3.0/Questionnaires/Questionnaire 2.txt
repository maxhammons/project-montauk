This clarifies the vision substantially. Two existing 3.0 decisions are now effectively superseded:

  - The machine is not purely a “dumb churner.” It has a scheduled, model-agnostic frontier agent that authors
    strategies, studies results, and restocks the pipeline. The Gold verdict remains deterministic.

  - There is no staging board or Trade Roster. Gold candidates automatically enter the leaderboard; only changing the
    active strategy requires human approval.

  My current one-sentence understanding is:

  > Montauk 3.0 is a personal, always-on TECL research appliance where an AI agent continuously invents executable
  > strategy candidates, a deterministic pipeline backtests and validates them, every qualifying Gold strategy
  > automatically joins the leaderboard, and Montauk recommends—but does not normally activate—a new leader without
  > Max’s approval.

  One important boundary needs to be explicit: no validation system can guarantee that a strategy is not overfit in an
  absolute sense or that its next call will be correct. Gold can guarantee that no overfitting was detected by Montauk’s
  versioned, reproducible validation suite. A strategy can still fail because markets change. If 3.0 is defined as
  “never makes a wrong call,” it is impossible to complete. If it is defined as “uses the strongest honest process
  available, exposes remaining weaknesses, and quickly demotes strategies when reality contradicts them,” it is
  buildable.
