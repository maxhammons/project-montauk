Questionnaire 7 - Research Reconciliation Decisions

This questionnaire contains the complete owner-decision inventory that remains
after reviewing the completed research package against Questionnaires 1-6. It does
not ask Max to select statistical algorithms blindly. Part A contains facts and
product policies that can be answered now. Part B records consequential settings
that Phase 1 must bring back with measured evidence; Max may approve the suggested
decision process now without selecting an arbitrary number. The research package
and active Montauk 3.0 documents remain unchanged until a later explicit
reconciliation pass.


Part A - Facts and product policies to answer now


1. Which broker and account interface will Max use for Montauk's market-on-open
orders?

Context. Max already decided to submit an order after the close for execution at
the next market open. The research found that participation in the NYSE Arca Core
Open Auction is broker- and order-type-specific. “Market order near 9:30” is not
necessarily the same fill as the auction print.

Suggested answer. Name the actual broker and interface, such as Schwab web/mobile,
Fidelity, or Interactive Brokers/TWS. Phase 1 then verifies the exact order type,
time-in-force, routing, partial-fill/cancellation behavior, and any account-specific
restriction before treating the auction fill as live-tradeable.

Reasoning. This is the last real-world fact needed to determine whether Gold's
modeled opening fill is obtainable.

Max's answer.


2. How should the real 2008-12-17 through 2012-06-28 TYH era enter Gold?

Context. The research found that the fund now called TECL originally traded as
TYH and targeted the Russell 1000 Technology Index. It changed ticker and benchmark
to the Technology Select Sector Index around 2012-06-29. Those are real, tradeable
fund returns, but they are not returns from the same benchmark contract TECL uses
today.

Suggested answer. Keep the TYH era as real observed fund evidence, but expose it as
a separate product era. Require the strategy to avoid catastrophic failure in that
era and report its era-specific B&H result; do not use the era to calibrate the
XLK-based synthetic reconstruction. Phase 1 should determine whether it is a hard
economic sub-gate or a required stress/generalization gate after measuring how much
the benchmark change affects otherwise sound controls.

Reasoning. Silently treating the fund as one unchanged product is wrong, while
discarding 3.5 years of actual tradable history also throws away evidence.

Max's answer.


3. Should a GitHub-only outage block an otherwise valid daily signal?

Context. This is narrower than the internet/power outage in Questionnaire 6.
Assume the Montauk computer is healthy, market data and the close are verified, the
signal is reproducible, and Max's notification channel works, but GitHub alone is
temporarily unavailable.

Suggested answer. No. Durably journal the signal locally, label the system
degraded_backup, alert Max, deliver the valid signal on time, and queue the GitHub
copy for verified retry. Block new Gold publication, Active changes, and other
nonessential authority changes until off-machine replication catches up.

Reasoning. A backup-provider outage should be visible, but it should not silently
turn into a trading rule or suppress a valid time-sensitive instruction.

Max's answer.


4. What disaster data-loss promise should 3.0 make while GitHub is the only
off-machine backup?

Context. An earlier answer required “no data loss,” while Questionnaire 6 says
GitHub is the backup for now and additional drives may come later. If the entire
tower is destroyed between GitHub syncs, a GitHub-only design cannot promise zero
loss. A second disk in the same tower protects against some disk/software failures,
but not fire, theft, or shared-power failure.

Suggested answer. For 3.0, require zero loss of already-acknowledged Active,
approval, signal, and Gold mutations against ordinary process/disk failure by using
a proven local journal plus a second local device. Explicitly accept a bounded
physical-disaster recovery point equal to the last verified GitHub sync until a
second offsite backup is added. Re-runnable in-progress research may restart from
the beginning, as already answered.

Reasoning. This turns “no data loss” into a promise the hardware can actually
support and names the one residual exception instead of hiding it.

Max's answer.


5. Must the dead-man alert be independent of Montauk's Slack/Buzz workspace?

Context. Separate read-only Slack/Buzz update channels are useful, but they are not
an independent dead-man path. If the host, home internet, or the shared messaging
provider is unavailable, Montauk cannot post the message that says it is dead.

Suggested answer. Yes. Keep Slack/Buzz as the only command-capable conversation,
and add one outbound-heartbeat service with no Montauk credentials that alerts by
an outside path such as email, SMS, or phone push when the daily heartbeat or signal
deadline is missed.

Reasoning. This adds no second authority channel; it only reports the absence of
the primary system.

Max's answer.


6. Can a later search-accounting discovery make an existing Gold certificate stale?

Context. The research lead states that future search never affects an issued Gold
certificate. That is too broad. A normal new candidate should not rewrite an old
certificate, but a later discovery that Montauk omitted relevant prior searches,
mis-clustered thousands of near-twins, leaked a holdout, or used an invalid
multiplicity method means the original Search Honesty plank may never have been
valid.

Suggested answer. Ordinary future searching does not change an old certificate.
A material search-ledger, holdout, lineage, or multiplicity defect does immediately
stale every affected certificate and triggers prioritized recertification under a
corrected signed contract, just like a material data or engine correctness defect.

Reasoning. An immutable certificate should preserve what was claimed and why; it
should not remain current Gold after evidence shows that one mandatory plank was
computed incorrectly.

Max's answer.


7. How should TECL cash distributions be handled for the strategy and matched B&H?

Context. The charter requires adjusted total-return TECL and a matched comparator,
but the current runtime credits a separate distribution ledger as cash. The ledger
starts in 2021 despite evidence of earlier distributions, credits cash by ex-date
rather than pay-date, does not reinvest it, and could double-count distributions if
the price series is later replaced with a total-return-adjusted series.

Suggested answer. Use one complete, primary-source distribution ledger. Recognize
entitlement on the ex-date but make cash available on the pay-date. Reinvest
distributions for both an invested strategy and B&H at the same first obtainable
price and cost convention, unless the strategy is risk-off on the reinvestment
date. Keep tradable OHLC, split-adjusted feature prices, and total-return wealth as
separate data views so distributions can never be omitted or counted twice.

Reasoning. “Adjusted total return” needs one explicit, symmetric convention before
Gold can honestly claim to beat matched B&H.

Max's answer.


8. May a signed core or validator release reset Montauk's search-history penalty?

Context. The research recommends one lifetime testing ledger but allows it to reset
after a signed core-version release. A software release does not make years of
searching the same TECL history disappear, and an automatic reset would be an easy
way to weaken the Search Honesty plank.

Suggested answer. No automatic reset. Every release retains lifetime search and
holdout provenance. A new signed epoch may use a separately justified allocation
only when the statistical contract explains why the old and new questions differ;
it never erases or hides the earlier search, and a renamed optimizer, campaign, or
feature source is not enough.

Reasoning. Versioning should identify changed methodology, not manufacture a fresh
untested history.

Max's answer.


9. What exact confirmation interaction should an opposite-state Active switch use?

Context. Questionnaire 6 asks for one-button switching after the proposed strategy,
improvement, and trade impact are shown. The ranking research instead recommends a
separate two-screen confirmation flow.

Suggested answer. Use one complete review card showing exact old/new strategy IDs,
both signals, whether the target state changes, the resulting next-open instruction,
performance/confidence differences, drawdown/catastrophe evidence, timestamps, and
expiry. After that information is already visible, one explicit button confirms
the switch. No default acceptance, timeout acceptance, or hidden second trade.

Reasoning. This preserves the requested one-button action without making a real
opposite-state instruction look like a harmless leaderboard pointer update.

Max's answer.


10. When should a dismissed or deferred recommendation be shown again?

Context. Questionnaire 6 approved dismiss/defer actions but did not define when the
same proposal becomes materially new rather than a repeated alert.

Suggested answer. A deferral resurfaces at its chosen expiry or after a material
Gold/integrity event. A dismissal remains quiet until the proposal clears a
versioned material-improvement threshold beyond the evidence already dismissed,
the current Active strategy weakens materially, or Max requests it. Phase 1 should
calibrate the exact improvement threshold from recommendation-churn tests rather
than installing the research report's arbitrary “50% of threshold or 20 bars.”

Reasoning. This preserves a quiet interface without allowing dismissal to hide a
meaningfully changed decision.

Max's answer.


11. Does a material TECL product change merely alert Max, or also suspend trusted
signals and stale Gold?

Context. Questionnaire 6 requires daily/weekly TECL news monitoring and immediate
alerts for closure or other large changes, but it does not explicitly say whether
old certificates remain current after the traded product changes.

Suggested answer. A split or ordinary distribution follows the tested corporate-
action path. A benchmark, leverage objective, ticker/CUSIP continuity, closure,
prolonged halt, or other material contract change triggers an immediate alert,
suspends new trusted signals once effective, stales every affected certificate,
and requires an owner-approved contract update and full recertification. Do not
automatically substitute another ETF.

Reasoning. A material fund change alters the object Montauk certified, not merely
one more daily observation.

Max's answer.


12. May Montauk perform a clean-machine restore drill even though production and
strategy testing remain on one computer?

Context. Questionnaire 6 correctly keeps production testing contained on one
computer. Recovery cannot be proven solely on that same computer, however; a
restore procedure that has never recreated the authority/Gold state elsewhere is
still hypothetical.

Suggested answer. Yes. Permit a periodic, isolated, non-authoritative restore onto
a spare machine or temporary VM using only the documented backups. It may verify
hashes, reconstruct the last trusted state, and run acceptance fixtures, but it may
not perform live discovery, publish Gold, send trusted signals, or become a second
authority. Destroy or reseal it after the drill.

Reasoning. This tests disaster recovery without creating off-machine production
testing or a hot standby.

Max's answer.


Part B - Consequential settings Phase 1 must return with evidence


13. What long-run false-Gold promise should Phase 1 optimize for?

Context. The current charter uses a one-percent annual probability of any false
Gold as an aspirational reference. Questionnaire 6 also requires five-year,
ten-year, and lifetime interpretations. The research package's proposed FDR/mFDR
wealth ledger does not prove a one-percent probability of no false Gold over the
appliance's lifetime.

Suggested answer. Preserve the annual figure as a provisional plotting reference,
not a promise. Require Phase 1 to compare methods that target the probability of
one or more false Golds, show annual/5-year/10-year/lifetime behavior and planted-
signal recovery under explicit assumptions, and recommend one understandable
long-run contract. Max selects the final operating point only after seeing that
frontier. Do not represent FDR/mFDR as the requested any-false-Gold guarantee.

Reasoning. The error definition must be correct before choosing its numerical
level.

Max's answer.


14. Should every hard real-data horizon require its own uncertainty-aware lower
bound, and should “insufficient” block Gold?

Context. Complete real history and trailing five years are hard economic gates.
The research leaves open whether the five-year gate gets only a point estimate and
whether one insufficient horizon may still count as passing.

Suggested answer. Apply the same uncertainty-aware treatment to every hard horizon.
If a required horizon is insufficient, Gold is blocked but the row says
insufficient rather than economically failed. Phase 1 may recommend changing a
window from hard gate to diagnostic if controls show that the window cannot provide
decision-grade evidence; it may not silently treat insufficient as a pass.

Reasoning. Calling a period a hard gate while allowing unsupported evidence to pass
would make “hard gate” meaningless.

Max's answer.


15. How should the final confidence level and evidence-sufficiency floors be set?

Context. The research recommends a one-sided 90% lower bound mainly because 95%
may leave the board empty. It also proposes regime, block-count, and MinTRL floors
that have not yet been validated for Montauk's terminal-wealth estimand.

Suggested answer. Do not ratify 90%, 95%, a minimum effective sample size, or a
minimum planted-signal recall rate now. Phase 1 must measure coverage, false Gold,
false rejection, and interval width on several known data-generating processes and
return one minimal recommended package in plain language. Max then approves the
operating point; current leaderboard survival is not an input.

Reasoning. Board fullness is a consequence to disclose, not a statistical reason
to weaken a confidence statement.

Max's answer.


16. May Phase 1 acquire paid official-close, auction, or quote data if free sources
cannot support the execution claim?

Context. The repo has daily aggregators but no official-close/SIP reference,
opening-auction imbalance history, or TECL quote data. Without them, Phase 1 can
only produce a conservative execution-cost range and cannot precisely validate the
auction fill assumption across the $10,000-$100,000 band.

Suggested answer. First exhaust free primary records, the actual broker's account
documentation, and a bounded live/shadow audit. If a specific unresolved Gold claim
still requires paid data, return the exact dataset, price, coverage, and decision it
would resolve for Max's approval. Until approved, use a documented conservative
range and label precise execution cost insufficient rather than inventing a point
estimate.

Reasoning. This prevents unapproved spending while keeping missing execution
evidence visible.

Max's answer.


17. Should a third Montauk Score pillar be admitted based on classifying control
worlds, or only on better ranking decisions among already-Gold rows?

Context. The Phase 1 research proposes admitting a pillar if it fixes one control's
Gold classification. Montauk Score ranks rows after Gold eligibility; it is not
supposed to become a hidden sixth Gold gate.

Suggested answer. Keep the two-pillar score unless a third input demonstrates
distinct, stable, out-of-sample ranking or switch-decision value among already-Gold
controls/rows without duplicating a hard gate. Never admit it merely because it
changes Gold classification. Any eligibility-relevant information belongs in an
explicit Gold plank instead.

Reasoning. Ranking and certification answer different questions and should not
silently absorb each other.

Max's answer.


18. How should the “leader not clearly separated” confidence level be selected?

Context. The ranking research proposes 90%, with an automatic fall to 75% if the
flag appears too often. Reducing confidence because a warning is inconvenient can
manufacture false rank precision.

Suggested answer. Keep the flag disclosure-only as already decided. Phase 1 should
calibrate its method and level against known equal and separated leaders, report
false-clear and false-ambiguity rates, and recommend one stable level. Do not lower
the level merely to meet a desired flag frequency.

Reasoning. Alert volume should improve presentation and retrigger policy, not alter
the meaning of statistical separation.

Max's answer.


19. When may the custom Rust strategy-module escape hatch be enabled?

Context. Declarative StrategySpec is the normal path. The research proposes a
zero-third-party-crate escape hatch plus compile/runtime containment, but adversarial
tests cannot prove containment and several policy details remain uncalibrated.

Suggested answer. Keep the escape hatch disabled by default. After the complete
compile-time and runtime containment suite, causal parity, deterministic replay,
resource-limit tests, dependency policy, signing, and recovery drill pass, present
one owner-review package. Only Max may explicitly enable that exact signed version;
a passing suite never enables it automatically.

Reasoning. This preserves novel-mechanism capability without making an exceptional
code path part of the trusted surface by default.

Max's answer.


20. When may Phase 1 install a catastrophic real-data risk veto?

Context. Questionnaire 6 approved studying a narrow catastrophic-loss/ruin floor,
not selecting an arbitrary maximum drawdown. The final adoption rule still needs
owner authority.

Suggested answer. Phase 1 must show the candidate veto's caught-defect rate, false-
rejection rate for sound trend strategies, exact economic interpretation, and why
ordinary drawdown disclosure is insufficient. Max approves or rejects the proposed
veto as a separate Gold-contract decision. If no defensible boundary emerges, no
veto is installed and the limitation remains prominent in activation review.

Reasoning. A catastrophic floor may be valuable, but it must not become a disguised
preference for smooth backtests.

Max's answer.


End of Questionnaire 7.
