# Montauk 3.0 Research — Stream 4: Economic gate and low-sample inference

## 1. One-page plain-English conclusion

**The problem in one sentence:** a 1.10× terminal share/wealth ratio over the complete ~17.5-year real TECL history and a 1.10× ratio over the trailing five years are not the same claim, and Montauk's economic gate must stop treating them as if they were.

Here is why, with numbers. Suppose Strategy A ends the complete real TECL history (real trading began 2008-12-17; see `data/TECL.csv` provenance columns) at exactly 1.10× B&H's terminal share count, and Strategy B ends the trailing five years at the same 1.10×. Converted to an annualized rate — `ln(1.10)/years` — Strategy A's edge is `ln(1.10)/17.5 ≈ 0.54%/yr` compounded, Strategy B's is `ln(1.10)/5 ≈ 1.91%/yr` compounded: **~3.5× larger annualized effect, compressed into ~3.5× fewer years.** That is a purely arithmetic statement about the two windows' *lengths* — it is **not** a claim that Strategy B rests on 3.5× less independent evidence. §6 is explicit that elapsed years do not scale linearly with independent regimes: the full history contains only ~3–4 independent macro/volatility regimes (`validation-engine-hardening.md` gap G3), and the trailing five years may be effectively **one dominant regime**, not "a fifth as many regimes." Horizon compression and regime scarcity are two separate concerns that happen to point the same direction here; collapsing them into one "3.5×" headline would smuggle in exactly the years-≈-evidence equivalence this report exists to reject. Two rows that look identical on the terminal ratio can still represent very different amounts of actual evidence — for two distinct reasons, both of which must be shown, not merged.

**What Max should decide Phase 1 must build**, concretely:

1. **Keep the terminal wealth/share multiple as the headline number** (already ratified in `decisions.md` D46) — do not change what the leaderboard leads with.
2. **Always pair it with a horizon-normalized annualized excess-return figure**, computed identically for every window, so a reviewer can see whether a "1.10×" is a slow multi-decade edge or a fast, thin, recent one.
3. **Never treat the point estimate as the decision.** Compute a one-sided lower confidence bound using a dependence-aware block bootstrap that **re-runs the actual strategy logic on resampled price paths** — not a resample of realized trades or daily P&L, which silently assumes entries/exits wouldn't change under a different history (false for any path-dependent, indicator-driven strategy). If the lower bound does not clear 1.0, the honest verdict is **insufficient evidence** — not "fail," not "pass."
4. **Report how much independent evidence actually exists** — regime count, effective decision count, minimum track record length — next to every ratio, every window, every time.
5. **Accept, in writing, that the trailing-five-year window alone may almost never produce statistically defensible evidence** at TECL's current sample size, because it likely spans too few independent regimes for any resampling method to manufacture a tight bound. That is not a bug in the statistics; it is the honest ceiling of ~17.5 years of one levered ETF's history (`validation-engine-hardening.md` G3: ~3–4 macro regimes, real-era multiple ~1.0–1.3×).

The rest of this report is the evidence and experiment design behind that recommendation.

## 3. Recommended Montauk experiments

Each experiment runs once per frozen candidate row (or once on a frozen control battery), with everything below the estimand fixed *before* results are inspected, per the charter's ban on tuning anchors toward the current pool.

### Experiment 4.1 — Horizon-normalized materiality alongside the terminal ratio

**Preregistered estimand.** For a frozen configuration and matched-B&H comparator, over each hard-gate window `w ∈ {complete_history, trailing_5y}`: `θ_w` = terminal wealth/share multiple vs. matched B&H (existing D46 estimand, unchanged); `δ_w = ln(θ_w) / years_w`, the horizon-normalized annualized excess log-return, using exact elapsed calendar years. Both are computed for every window, every row, always — a deterministic transform of numbers the backtest already produces, not a new statistical test.

**Stopping rule.** None beyond "compute once per frozen row per certification epoch" — arithmetic on an already-frozen backtest carries no sequential-inspection risk. Freeze the formula (`ln(θ)/years`, ACT/ACT calendar years) in the validator contract version before applying it to any existing row, so it cannot be adjusted after seeing which rows it flatters.

**What it changes.** The leaderboard shows both numbers, both windows. A reviewer comparing two 1.10× rows sees immediately whether one implies a 0.5%/yr edge over 17+ years or a 1.9%/yr edge inferred from five — materially different claims that must not share one sentence.

### Experiment 4.2 — Dependence-aware lower bound via full causal path resimulation

**Preregistered estimand.** The one-sided `(1−α)` lower confidence bound `θ_LB,w` on the terminal ratio for window `w`: the empirical `α`-quantile of `θ_w^(b)` across `B` replicates, where each replicate (1) draws a resampled daily price path via the **stationary bootstrap** (Politis & Romano 1994) with block length from the corrected **automatic selector** (Patton, Politis & White 2009), computed once per window and frozen (not hand-tuned); (2) **reruns the complete causal strategy engine** — indicators, entries/exits, position state, cooldown, slippage — on that path from scratch, exactly as `validation-engine-hardening.md` §2a requires ("reconstruct price paths and rerun the full causal strategy — not merely resample already-realized trades or daily P&L"); (3) recomputes matched-B&H terminal shares on the *same* resampled path; (4) records `θ_w^(b)` = strategy shares / B&H shares.

**Stopping rule.** `B` (e.g. 2,000–5,000) and `α` (e.g. one-sided 90%, see §8) are frozen in the validator contract *before* running on any real candidate. Run to exactly `B`; never stop early because the running quantile already clears or fails 1.0.

**Decision rule (owner-facing).** `θ_LB,w > 1.0` → economic passage for `w`. `θ_LB,w ≤ 1.0` → **insufficient evidence** for `w` — distinct from "fails": a true edge can exist and still be unresolvable at this sample size.

### Experiment 4.3 — Effective evidence accounting (regimes, decisions, MinTRL)

**Preregistered estimand.** For each frozen row/window: (a) count of independent macro/volatility regimes the window spans (existing bull/bear segmentation, not a redefinition); (b) an effective sample size with units made explicit — `N_eff = trading_days_w / block_length_w`, where both quantities are in **trading days**, the selector's native unit (dividing elapsed *years* by a day-denominated block length is dimensionally invalid — e.g. `17.5 years / 20 days ≈ 0.875` is not a plausible effective-block count — so the unit must be stamped in the validator contract alongside the number); (c) a Minimum Track Record Length **cross-walked** from Bailey & López de Prado's Sharpe-ratio formulation to Montauk's log-wealth-difference statistic, documented step-by-step as a derivation, not borrowed as a plug-in, since the source formula targets a different estimand.

**Stopping rule.** The cross-walk and the regime/`N_eff` estimator are frozen and versioned once, in Phase 1, on the predeclared control battery (§4) — never re-derived per candidate.

**Decision rule.** Regime count `< 2`, or `N_eff` below a to-be-calibrated floor, or MinTRL exceeding available real history by more than a predeclared margin → **insufficient evidence** for that window regardless of `θ_w` or `θ_LB,w`, stated in plain language on the row.

### Experiment 4.4 — Redundancy/complementarity calibration of the two hard gates

**Preregistered estimand.** Using the control worlds from `validation-engine-hardening.md` §3e (dependence-preserving nulls, exposure-matched random rules, planted-signal worlds, structural controls): the joint pass-rate table of {complete-history gate, trailing-5y gate}, specifically (a) "recency-only" false positives (passes 5y, fails complete-history) and (b) "stale-regime" false negatives (fails 5y, passes complete-history, on a control with a genuine current-regime edge).

**Stopping rule.** Run once on the complete predeclared battery; freeze the read as one Phase 1 report; never re-tune the rolling-window rule or catastrophic-window veto against real candidates afterward.

## 4. Null, defect, and planted-signal controls

- **Zero-edge dependence-preserving null.** Simulate a TECL-like process (matching volatility clustering/autocorrelation) with true edge exactly zero. Expected: `δ_w` centers near 0, and `θ_LB,w > 1.0` occurs at ~the frozen `α` rate, no more — a materially higher rate flags the resimulation as too permissive.
- **Planted, recoverable small edge.** Inject a known, exactly-computable drift differential into a synthetic path. Expected: `θ_LB,w` clears 1.0 with power degrading gracefully as the edge shrinks — operationalizing gap G6 (small-sample power) as a measured number, and telling Max plainly whether the five-year window can detect a realistic edge at all.
- **High-dimensional noise family, searched at Montauk's actual scale.** Run the full author/search/select pipeline over thousands of zero-true-edge configurations, apply the full economic gate to survivors. Expected: this is a **joint test with Stream 2's multiplicity correction** — the economic gate alone should not be expected to fully suppress false Gold here.
- **Trade-resampling-vs-path-resimulation parity fixture.** On a strongly path-dependent strategy (ATR trailing stop, Donchian filter), run both the correct method (resample price, rerun engine) and the deliberately wrong one (resample realized P&L). Expected: the two produce **materially different** CIs (the wrong method typically narrower/falsely tight, since it freezes decisions that should vary under resampling). Close agreement is itself a red flag of silent degeneration.
- **Degenerate-window control.** A strategy always-in or always-out for the full trailing five years. Expected: regime/decision count registers ~0–1, and the row reports **insufficient evidence**, not a spurious pass from one unbroken position.

## 5. False-Gold and false-rejection consequences

**False Gold from a thin, non-annualized recent number.** If a 1.10× trailing-five-year ratio stands unqualified, Max may activate a strategy whose apparent edge is one lucky post-2022 stretch restated as "five years of evidence." The cost is real capital exposure to weaker support than the headline implies, and — per the ratified priority order ("a false Gold admission is considered slightly worse than rejecting a strategy that might have been good," `charter.md` §2.3) — a real, if deliberately *marginal* rather than overwhelming, cost in trust once discovered. The charter frames this as a mild tiebreaker, not a categorical prohibition; this report treats it the same way: both error types are costs to minimize, not one acceptable and the other not.

**False rejection from over-conservative calibration.** If Experiment 4.2's `α` or Experiment 4.3's evidence floor is set too strictly, a genuinely adapted, currently-live edge could be permanently blocked because five (or seventeen) years of one levered ETF cannot produce a tight bootstrap interval — not because the edge is fake, but because TECL's data ceiling is real. The cost is an empty or near-empty board, forcing manual, un-certified execution or a stale champion.

**The asymmetry is not Stream 4's to resolve unilaterally.** Per D50/D57, Max reviews the false-Gold/false-reject frontier and chooses the operating point after seeing it; Stream 4's job is to make that frontier visible and computable (§4's control battery), not to pre-select a value favoring a fuller or emptier board.

## 6. Assumptions and power/limits

- **Real TECL history is ~17.5 years** (4,401 real rows verified against `data/TECL.csv`), implying at most ~3–4 independent regimes (G3). No resampling or HAC method manufactures degrees of freedom the data don't contain.
- **The trailing five-year window (~2021–2026) likely spans one to two regime transitions** (2021 momentum, 2022 rate-shock drawdown, 2023–2026 AI rally) — for a long-holding trend strategy this may be effectively **one dominant regime**. Single-window resampling tests on this slice alone should be expected to frequently return "insufficient evidence," honestly, and Phase 1 should state that expectation up front.
- **Daily observation counts overstate effective sample size.** Lo (2002) shows this mechanically for the Sharpe ratio; the same caution applies to any daily log-wealth-difference statistic here. Thousands of bars from a <20-trade, multi-week-hold strategy represent far fewer independent decisions than the bar count suggests.
- **Stationarity is an assumption, not a fact.** Block-bootstrap validity rests on (weak) stationarity. TECL/XLK's regime history (2008 crisis, 2020 COVID shock, 2022 rate shock, 2023–2026 AI rally) is not obviously stationary across its full span; Phase 1 should disclose sensitivity via a small predeclared grid of alternate block lengths, not present one point estimate as settled.
- **Synthetic pre-2008 history cannot manufacture additional real regimes.** 3,937 of 8,338 `TECL.csv` rows are synthetic (pre-inception); per D30/D47 these remain diagnostic-only and may not inflate the regime count used in Experiment 4.3.
- **Champion-scale strategies have <20 trades.** Trade-level bootstrap/MinTRL methods at this count give wide, often uninformative bounds. Report interval width and effective-N plainly rather than force a falsely narrow interval through an inapplicable parametric assumption (the honest analogue of gap G6).
- **The MinTRL/DSR formula targets a Sharpe ratio, not Montauk's terminal ratio.** Using it without an explicit cross-walk (4.3) would silently answer a question Montauk isn't asking.
- **`N_eff`'s unit convention must be fixed and stamped, not left implicit.** The selector natively returns block length in trading days; `N_eff` is only well-defined as `trading_days_w / block_length_w`, both in days. Writing it as `years_w / block_length_w` without stating units is dimensionally wrong and would silently produce a nonsense figure. Experiment 4.3 fixes this; Phase 1 must add a unit assertion in code so this class of error cannot recur silently.
- **Two of seven cited papers (Politis & Romano 1994; Newey & West 1987) could not be independently fetched** — both 403/paywalled, no open-access mirror found; marked `[Inference, secondary-sourced]` in §2, not a direct primary-text read. Disclosed gap, not a resolved one — see §8 for the owner decision on whether to gate implementation on completing this check. The DSR "≈3.26 at 1,000 trials" figure's exact paper-of-origin is similarly unconfirmed from primary text (§2, row 4) — Stream 2's concern, not load-bearing here.

## 7. Required fixtures and durable artifacts

- **Constant-edge invariance fixture.** A synthetic price series with an exactly known, constant annualized drift differential vs. its matched benchmark, evaluated at two lengths (5y and 17.5y of the same constant edge). Expected: `δ_w` recovers the known rate to numerical precision at both lengths, proving horizon-normalization is correctly implemented and window-length-invariant when the true edge is constant.
- **Hindsight-collapse fixture.** A synthetic "strategy" holding exactly through the best historical stretch and sitting out the worst. Expected: `θ_w` looks strong, but `θ_LB,w` (4.2) collapses to ≤1.0 under path resimulation — proving the lower bound actually detects hindsight-driven point estimates rather than restating them.
- **Trade-resampling-vs-path-resimulation parity artifact.** Retain, for one path-dependent reference strategy, both the correct (engine rerun on resampled paths) and incorrect (resampled realized P&L) CI outputs side by side, permanently, as audit evidence the implementation was not silently downgraded to the cheaper wrong shortcut.
- **MinTRL/`N_eff` worked-example regression fixture.** A frozen, hand-computable numeric example, independent of the Rust engine, checked against Bailey & López de Prado's published worked example — a golden-value regression test for the cross-walked formula.
- **Per-row retained artifacts** (every frozen Gold or Pending-Gold row): (a) the full bootstrap replicate distribution, not only the summary bound; (b) the exact selected block length (with unit) and its selection diagnostic; (c) `θ_w` and `δ_w` for every hard-gate window; (d) regime/`N_eff`/MinTRL figures and the method version; (e) a plain-English trace of which windows resolved pass / fail / insufficient-evidence and why.

## 8. Unresolved owner decisions

- **Confidence level for the resimulated lower bound (`α`, Experiment 4.2).** *Recommended default:* one-sided 90%. **[Inference/judgment call, not statistically derived]** — unlike the corrected block-length formula or the horizon-normalization transform, 90% vs. 95% vs. 99% is not something the data or cited papers pick; it is a stated opinion about an acceptable false-Gold/false-reject operating point, using the same "recommended default" language this report uses elsewhere for genuinely derived choices. *Tradeoff:* 95% (the more conventional level in most financial-inference contexts) will very likely return "insufficient evidence" for almost every candidate at TECL's ~17.5-year, ~3–4-regime ceiling, risking a perpetually near-empty board; 90% admits more real signal at higher false-Gold risk. Read this number as a business-continuity argument, not a statistical fact, when ratifying it.
- **Should the trailing-five-year gate get its own uncertainty-aware lower bound, or remain a point-estimate check subordinate to the full-history gate?** *Recommended default:* apply the same treatment symmetrically to both windows. *Tradeoff:* makes the 5y gate very hard to clear alone (single-regime problem, §6) vs. a two-tier design (full-history = hard gate; 5y = disclosed point-estimate diagnostic) that is more permissive but reintroduces the "impressive but unsupported recent number" risk this stream exists to prevent.
- **How should "insufficient evidence" on one window (clean pass on the other) be represented for Gold eligibility?** *Recommended default:* treat `insufficient` as non-passing (blocks Gold) but visually distinct from a hard fail, per D50's mandate that evidence sufficiency replace a blunt cliff. *Tradeoff:* stricter, shrinks the board, vs. letting `insufficient` count as a soft pass, trading rigor for a fuller board.
- **One frozen block length over the whole history, or era-specific block lengths/regime boundaries?** *Recommended default:* one automatically-selected length over the complete real series, with a disclosed sensitivity check across a small predeclared grid. *Tradeoff:* simpler, resistant to after-the-fact tuning, vs. a design that may understate uncertainty at regime transitions.
- **Sharpe-based MinTRL as published, or a fully derived cross-walk to Montauk's estimand?** *Recommended default:* derive and document the cross-walk before using any MinTRL-style number in a Gold decision. *Tradeoff:* more Phase-1 work up front vs. a faster but estimand-mismatched number that could mislead Max about what "insufficient evidence" means for the quantity he actually cares about — terminal shares, not a Sharpe ratio.
- **Two cited papers remain unverified against primary text (§2, §6).** *Recommended default:* complete a full-text read of Politis & Romano (1994) and Newey & West (1987), via institutional access or an alternate mirror, before Phase 1 freezes the block-bootstrap implementation on their authority alone. *Tradeoff:* the methods are textbook-standard and low-risk to proceed on provisionally, but a document meant to freeze a preregistered formula should not rest evidence ratings on unread primary sources — Max should decide whether to gate implementation on this check or proceed and backfill it.
